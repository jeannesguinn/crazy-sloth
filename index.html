ABOUT ME

A born creative, my career began as a software designer with Advanced Micro Devices, where I learned how to solve complex problems. My eye for design then led me to embark on a new journey as a jewelry designer. Jewelry, like software, requires a level of detail oriented-ness as individual bits come together to form one beautiful solution.

Although I enjoyed the creativity in jewelry design, I wanted to challenge myself with new avenues of design + innovation. It was at this point that I dove head first into two of General Assembly’s full time programs. I enrolled in UX Design first – five days a week for 10 weeks – 400+ hours of instruction. From there, I continued into their web development class – again, five days a week, but for 12 weeks – more than 500 hours of instruction. I loved every minute of both courses and embrace every chance I get to utilize my newfound skill sets.

BANDMEETER

Timeframe: 2 weeks (class project)

Project Overview

Guitar Center wants to establish itself as a major player in the digital space by offering a way for local musicians to connect and collaborate on a new digital platform. 

MY ROLE

I worked as a part of a three-person team for this class project. Each portion of the project was highly dependent on our close collaboration and utilizing each team member's unique strengths. 

Guitar Center has lost its following due to poor customer experiences and its big box feel. They need to find a way to re-connect to their roots, focusing on local musicians and music community.

DISCOVERY
&
USER RESEARCH

Competitive Analysis

We conducted a Competitive Analysis in order to find out what the digital landscape looked like for local musicians looking to share music, network, collaborate & book gigs.  

What We Learned

We learned that most of the current sites for musicians looking to collaborate, book gigs, network or share music charged an expensive monthly fee. The sites we researched were not focused on fostering the local music scene. 

The current competitor sites weren't satisfying local musician's needs and were generally focused on one main task, such as selling music or booking gigs. There was a real need for a digital platform that could place emphasis on the multiple needs of local musicians.

USER RESEARCH

Field Research & User Interviews

We went out into the field in order to find out how & where musicians:

  • Promote & Share Music
  • Use Remote Collaboration
  • Purchase Music Equipment
  • Find People to Play Music with
  • Find Venues to Play Music

PERSONA DEVELOPMENT

After going out into the field and conducting interviews and surveys, we put our noses to the grindstone and began to develop three very unique User Personas that could utilize Bandmeeter. These Personas were based around data collected from Contextual Analysis in the field, User Surveys, Interviews with Peer Musicians, various local music venues, coffee shops, record shops and instrument shops. All of our user research was solely focused on the local music community, in Austin, Texas.

EMPATHY MAPPING

One of our first stops on our journey to developing Bandmeeter's User Personas was empathy mapping. This exercise helped us to consider what each user was thinking, feeling, doing, seeing and hearing on a day to day basis (as it relates to their musical desires). Empathy mapping allowed us to discover possible pain-points and challenges that could emerge as they engaged with Bandmeeter.

SKETCHING & IDEATION

Rough Sketches

After hashing out the details of the Bandmeeter User Personas, we put pen to paper and sketched out some rough user flows. 

NARROWING SCOPE & STRUCTURE

Card Sorting & Defining the MVP

We performed a Card Sorting Activity in order to narrow down our scope and define our Minimum Viable Product.  The sort allowed us to define what elements and features were the most important to our users. We structured our MVP into an Agile Workflow, in the interest of time. Our first sprint was comprised of features we must have in order to be useful to our users. The second sprint included features our users would like to have, but were not necessarily a must have at this point in time. Our third, and final sprint, included features to be implemented in the future. These were the "bells and whistles" for our users. 

Defining the MVP

The Bandmeeter Sitemap

The Card Sorting Activity gave us insight into creating terminology and categories that resonated with our users. Based on user testing, we were able to confidently craft a site map for Bandmeeter.

Bandmeeter's Sitemap

Wireframes

We began to drill down on the details for our interface, via sketching and iterating those sketches around user feedback. The sketch below shows a possible interface for a band utilizing Bandmeeter to find a new band member. Originally, we thought about adding a feature that would allow users to add onto each other’s recorded tracks. Due to our two-week time constraint, we decided this would be a future feature that could be implemented into the interface.

Prototypes & Testing
